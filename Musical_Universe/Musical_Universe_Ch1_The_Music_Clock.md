## Gray — Standard Music Theory Reference

Equal temperament divides the octave into 12 semitones, each a fixed
frequency ratio apart: f_n = f_0 * 2^(n/12). This is real, standard,
centuries-refined music theory — the basis of virtually all Western
instrumental tuning since the 18th century. Chords, keys, and the
circle of fifths are all built on top of this real structure.

## The Music Clock

E-510's real definition: the Music Clock is a rotational coordinate
system for harmonic relationships, built directly on A-111's real
12-tone relation (f_n = f_0*2^(n/12)) — not a new tuning system, a
coordinate system laid on top of the existing real one.

Structure: 12 positions around a clock face, A at 12 o'clock.
Clockwise = positive = Expression (per B-203's real Expression node).
Counter-clockwise = negative = Compression (per B-204's real
Compression node). This connects music directly to this framework's
own compression/expression axis, rather than treating "sharp" and
"flat" as arbitrary labels.

## Mathematics

f_n = f_0 * (2^(n/12))  [A-111's real relation, unmodified]

Clock position: each of the 12 semitones assigned a signed integer
position from -6 to +6 (or equivalently 1-12 with a defined zero),
with clockwise motion mapping to positive/Expression and
counter-clockwise to negative/Compression.

## Predictions

If this mapping is real and not just decorative, chord qualities
(major vs. minor) should show up as genuinely different, checkable
signed positions on this clock — not just different names. This is
tested directly in Chapter 2 (Chord Rotation) and Chapter 3 (Leaning),
using this chapter's coordinate system as the foundation both build on.

## Yellow Audit

- The specific choice of A at 12 o'clock is a convention, not a

- Whether clockwise = Expression and counter-clockwise = Compression
