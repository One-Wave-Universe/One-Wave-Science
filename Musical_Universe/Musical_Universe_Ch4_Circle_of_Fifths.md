## Gray — Standard Music Theory Reference

The circle of fifths is one of the oldest and most standard tools in
music theory — a real, centuries-old ordering of the 12 keys by
ascending perfect fifths (C-G-D-A-E-B-F#-Db-Ab-Eb-Bb-F-back to C),
used for key relationships, chord progressions, and modulation. Not
invented here — this chapter formalizes it in this framework's own
rotation language.

## Section 1 — The Fifths Clock

A SEPARATE system from Chapter 1's Music Clock, explicitly not the
same ordering — E-510's clock is chromatic (semitone order); this is
fifths order. Confusing the two would be a real error; they are kept
distinct deliberately.

12 positions, ordered by ascending fifths: C(12)-G(1)-D(2)-A(3)-E(4)-
B(5)-F#/Gb(6)-Db(7)-Ab(8)-Eb(9)-Bb(10)-F(11)-back to C(12).
Clockwise (+1/step) = add a sharp, up a fifth: brighter, forward.
Counter-clockwise (-1/step) = add a flat, down a fifth: heavier,
grounding.
6 o'clock = the tritone position — maximum distance from home,
maximum instability, real and standard in music theory (the tritone's
harmonic instability is well-established, not a claim invented here).

## Section 2 — Functional Leaning (Distinct From Chapter 3's Leaning)

This is a DIFFERENT kind of leaning than Chapter 3's Chord Leaning
Direction — that was about a single chord's internal asymmetry;
this is about a chord's FUNCTIONAL ROLE within a key:

Tonic (I): the ground state, 12 o'clock, zero stored tension —
matches A-101's real Ground/Zero concept directly (a real, non-
coincidental connection, not just a naming similarity).
Subdominant (IV): outward lean, one step counter-clockwise (11
o'clock) — departs from home without urgency.
Dominant (V): inward gravitational pull, one step clockwise (1
o'clock) — maximum functional tension, wanting to resolve back to I.
This matches A-105's real Restoring Response concept directly: the
Dominant's "pull back to Tonic" is structurally the same shape as a
restoring force pulling a displaced system back to equilibrium.

## Mathematics

Fifths Clock position: assign each of the 12 keys a signed position
based on its distance (in fifths) from the tonic, distinct from the
Music Clock's chromatic positions.

## Predictions

If the Tonic/Subdominant/Dominant mapping to Ground-Zero/Restoring-
Response is real and not just a naming coincidence, real chord
progressions should show a checkable pattern: progressions ending on
V should show measurably higher "tension" (by some independent
measure) than those ending on IV, matching the different lean
magnitudes assigned here (Dominant's one-step pull vs. Subdominant's
one-step departure) — not yet tested against real listener or
harmonic-analysis data.
