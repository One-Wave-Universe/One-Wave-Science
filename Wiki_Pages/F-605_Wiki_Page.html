Definition

Phase-dependent combination that can reinforce, reduce, or cancel depending on the phase relationship φ between the two states.

Mathematics

ψ₁ = A·cos(ωt) ψ₂ = A·cos(ωt+φ) ψ_T = 2A·cos(φ/2)·cos(ωt+φ/2) A_eff = 2A·|cos(φ/2)| φ=0: full reinforcement  |  φ=π: full cancellation
Effective combined amplitude as phase difference phi sweeps from 0 to 2pi — full reinforcement at phi=0, full cancellation at phi=pi.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

Sibling of F-604 under the same two upstream nodes (Transfer, Interaction Differential) — Resonance is the φ=0 special case of Interference's general phase-dependent formula. The two nodes could arguably be unified with Resonance as a boundary condition of this one, rather than two separate parallel nodes; worth flagging for a future duplicate-vs-scale-instance check under I-04.

Recursive Reframing

The continuous waveform here already has period structure, which is inherently repeatable/recursive. A discrete recursive form would step the phase itself: φn+1 = φn + Δφ, recomputing A_eff each step — useful if φ is dynamically evolving rather than fixed, which is explicitly flagged below as unresolved.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

What physically sets φ in a given interaction is not derived (CCD-04). Whether φ is fixed or dynamically evolving is unresolved. Whether full cancellation (φ=π) is exact or approximate in the real lattice is unresolved.
