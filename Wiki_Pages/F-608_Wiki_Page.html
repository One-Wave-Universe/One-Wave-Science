Definition

The progressive weakening of state strength as it propagates or interacts — amplitude decreases with distance or time.

Mathematics

dA/dx = −μ·A, μ>0 A(x) = A₀ · exp(−μx) μ depends on: lattice resistance γ, coupling β, mode frequency
Continuous exponential decay (blue) versus the recursive per-step version (orange, dashed) — they converge, but the recursive form matches the repo's own discrete update-rule convention.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

Pulls from three separate upstream nodes at once (Transfer's exchange, Interference's cancellation, Reflection's boundary loss) — Attenuation is effectively what's left after all three other loss mechanisms have already acted, making it the most downstream-dependent node in the appendix.

Recursive Reframing

This is the clearest recursive opportunity in all of Appendix F. The node states the continuous solution (A(x)=A₀e^(−μx)) but the underlying assumption (dA/dx=−μA) is already a rate law, and the repo's own core update rule is discrete-step by design. The natural recursive restatement: An+1 = An · exp(−μ·Δx) per lattice step, which converges to the same continuous curve (shown as the orange stepped line in the graph, overlaid on the smooth continuous curve) but matches how every other A-series node in this repo actually computes forward in discrete steps. This should probably become the node's primary stated form, with the continuous solution kept as the derived limit rather than the other way around.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

Proportional decay assumption not yet verified for the One-Wave lattice specifically. μ not yet derived from γ and β. Frequency-dependence and non-exponential forms not ruled out.
