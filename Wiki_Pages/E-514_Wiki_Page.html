Definition

A SEPARATE fifths-ordered clock (not E-510's chromatic clock) tracking a chord's functional role within a key: Tonic(ground, 12 o'clock), Subdominant(outward lean, 11 o'clock), Dominant(inward pull, 1 o'clock).

Mathematics

12 positions by ascending fifths: C-G-D-A-E-B-F#/Gb-Db-Ab-Eb-Bb-F-C. 6 o'clock=tritone=maximum instability.
The fifths clock, distinct from E-510's chromatic clock -- Tonic at 12, Dominant one step clockwise, Subdominant one step counter-clockwise.

Math Trail

Explicitly and carefully disambiguated from E-510's chromatic clock (different ordering, different scope: per-chord vs. per-key) AND from E-513's different 'leaning' concept -- two real naming collisions resolved by keeping both concepts, not merging them.

Recursive Reframing

The Tonic-Subdominant-Dominant-Reset cycle is a real recursive songwriting pipeline, loosely mapped to B-221's six steps (Begin-Move-Hold-Build-Break-Loop) -- explicitly reframed this session as 'may instantiate,' not 'equals,' after an earlier version overstated the connection.

Yellow Audit

Entirely disconnected from the field-equation layer (A-series, update rule) -- this is formalized music theory, not derived physics-analog math, and should not be cited as if it were. The candidate B-221 correspondence is unverified.
