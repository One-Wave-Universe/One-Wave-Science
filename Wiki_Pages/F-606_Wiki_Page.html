Definition

The rejection and return of an incoming state at a boundary. An incoming mode splits into reflected and transmitted components.

Mathematics

A_i = A_r + A_t r = A_r / A_i, 0 ≤ r ≤ 1 A_r = r · A_i
Reflection fraction r and transmission fraction t=1-r as a function of boundary mismatch — together they always sum to 1 (gray dotted line), the conservation signature shared with F-607.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

The first node in Appendix F where a physical boundary enters the picture, rather than two states interacting freely. Draws on both Resonance and Interference (the incoming and boundary states can align or interfere before the split happens), and hands off directly to F-607's complementary transmission math.

Recursive Reframing

A single reflection event is a one-shot split, but a mode crossing many boundaries (a lattice of cells, for instance) reflects and transmits repeatedly. That's a natural recursive chain: A_in+1 = tn · A_in (what got through boundary n becomes the incoming amplitude at boundary n+1), with A_rn = rn·A_in lost at each step. This is exactly the shape needed to model attenuation through a multi-cell lattice as compounding reflections rather than one smooth exponential curve.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

What determines r at a given boundary is not yet derived. Whether r depends on frequency, angle, boundary type, or lattice coupling β is unresolved.
