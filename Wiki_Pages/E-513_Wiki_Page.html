Definition

A chord's Oscillation Window has a directional lean when |P_compress|≠|P_expr| -- one side pulls harder. Distinct from E-514's different 'leaning' concept (functional role within a key) despite the shared word.

Mathematics

L = Σ|negative positions| − Σ|positive positions|. A Major: L=1 (mild backward). A Minor: L=2 (stronger backward). L>0 backward/compression, L<0 forward/expression.
Both tested chords lean backward (L>0), Minor more strongly than Major -- 2 data points, not yet a rule.

Math Trail

The generalized L formula sums over ALL tones on each side, not just one pair -- meaning it extends cleanly to seventh/extended chords without needing a special case per chord type, a real design improvement over the original two-point version.

Recursive Reframing

Power chords are explicitly UNDEFINED (not undetermined) under this formula, since it needs at least one tone per side to sum -- a genuine, honestly-stated scope boundary rather than a forced answer.

Yellow Audit

Only 2 data points (same limitation as E-512). Power-chord 'flip to forward-leaning' claim is stated, NOT derived -- the primary open item. Whether minor always leans more backward than major is a real, currently unconfirmed pattern.
