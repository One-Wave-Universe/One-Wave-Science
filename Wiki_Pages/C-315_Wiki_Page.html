Definition

A sensor treating signal and noise as field states rather than separate categories, using differential nulling between two mirrored, out-of-phase emitters. Hypothesis: a stable field source creates a non-linear 'distortion tail' standard theory doesn't predict.

Mathematics

Derived this session, not just named: CMRR target ~120dB, phase-matching target ~0.08 degrees, for resolving a hypothetical residual at 1e-6 of emitter amplitude. Real proposed topology: single master oscillator, 180-degree splitter, matched differential amp, 24-bit ADC, digital lock-in detection.
The proposed measurement chain from master oscillator through lock-in detection -- a real, buildable circuit design, not yet built or tested.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

This is the first Wave Reader specification anywhere in the repo -- referenced as future hardware across many Book 1 chapters (Ch4, Ch6, Ch10, Ch13) for years of citations without ever being specified until this node.

Recursive Reframing

The lock-in/synchronous detection stage is inherently recursive (each cycle references the master oscillator's running phase) -- already the right structure for this kind of measurement, not a candidate for reframing.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

The 'distortion tail' claim has no connection yet to any real field equation beyond general plausibility. Inherits C-313's unresolved Lorentz conflict: if there's a real preferred frame, that changes what a 'null point' measurement is actually looking for. Whether any residual actually appears is the experiment itself, not something the topology can presuppose.
