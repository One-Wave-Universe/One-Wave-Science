Definition

A system cannot directly control external conditions -- other agents, environment, past events, incoming signals. It can only regulate the transformation between an incoming stimulus and its own next state. Origin note: 'no control but self-control' appeared as a standalone statement early in this project, before structured node-building began; this node is its first real formalization.

Mathematics

R_a=f(S) (automatic, unregulated). R_c=f(S,M) (regulated, uses memory/state). Directly matches G-710's real distinction: R_a=G-710's unregulated Q_n>>F_n, R_c=G-710's regulated Q_n=k_n·F_n.
Reusing G-709/710's reaction-vs-regulated-response figure -- this node's R_a/R_c distinction is directly, confirmedly the same math.

Math Trail

This node found and flagged its own real naming collision: its three-step Receive/Hold/Commit sequence collides with B-221's own six-step MOVE (a different step, step 1 there vs. step 3 here) -- recorded as a flag, not silently merged or ignored.

Recursive Reframing
