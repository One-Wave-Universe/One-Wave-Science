Four-dimensional layer

Projection Rule

Let (X_D) be a native state in dimension (D), and let

[ P_{Dd}:X_DX_d, d<D, ]

be a declared projection.

A projected quantity (Q) is valid only when either

[ Q(X_D)=Q_d(P_{Dd}X_D) ]

for an invariant quantity, or the projection error is explicitly measured:

[ Q = |Q(X_D)-Q_d(P{Dd}X_D)|. ]

A visually accurate projection is not automatically dynamically accurate.

Simulation Rule

A 2D simulation may test sixfold planar transfer, cell-area change, ring circulation, and overlap geometry. It may not claim to have derived a complete 3D knot.

A 3D simulation may test volumetric recurrence, knot geometry, internal/external shell relations, and twelvefold local coordination. It may not claim a complete 4D recurrence from one static frame.

A 4D simulation must preserve the ordered history of the evolving 3D state, including phase, Mirror crossings, direction, return path, and recurrence identity.

Failure Conditions

This node fails if:

  1. a lower-dimensional representation reproduces every native higher-dimensional degree of freedom without loss and an explicit isomorphism is derived;
  2. the canonical 6:1 or 12:1 coordination is replaced by a different native geometry that performs better under the repository’s tests;
  3. 24:1 is physically derived as a different structure than the completed Field/Void recurrence shell.

Until one of those results exists, dimensional separation is mandatory.
