TERMINOLOGY CORRECTION (this session): the symbol was previously F_OW (“Force, One-Wave”), inherited across A-106, A-107, B-201, B-221, C-305, C-310, and Book 1 Ch12. This was a real inconsistency, not a style choice — Book 1 Ch12 explicitly and repeatedly denies force-carrier mechanisms as fundamental, while this node’s own symbol called its output a “Force.” Renamed to R_OW (“Response, One-Wave”), which now matches this node’s actual title. The mechanism itself is unchanged — only the symbol and the word “Force” as a category name were corrected, everywhere they appeared as an affirmed One-Wave mechanism. Standard Model “force” and “force-carrier” language in Book 1’s Gray sections and explicit rejection passages (e.g. Ch1, Ch2, Ch7 — “no force-carrier needed”) was left untouched, since that usage is either accurately describing the comparison framework or correctly naming the thing being denied, not affirming a One-Wave force.
