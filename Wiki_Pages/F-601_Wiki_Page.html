Definition

A change in one bounded state produces a change in another. Without influence, no interaction can occur — it is the precondition for everything else in Appendix F.

Mathematics

ψ₂ = f(ψ₁) α = f'(ψ₁) Δψ₂ = α · Δψ₁ α=0 → no influence α≠0 and Δψ₁≠0 → Δψ₂≠0 (influence exists)
delta_psi_2 vs delta_psi_1 for several values of alpha — alpha=0 is the flat line (no influence); steeper lines mean stronger coupling.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

F-601 is the root of Appendix F — it has no F-series upstream, only A-112 (Persistent Mode) above it. Every other F-node's math assumes influence already holds; F-601 is the switch that turns interaction on or off.

Recursive Reframing

This is a single static coefficient right now (α fixed). A recursive form would let influence itself update step by step as the two states co-evolve: ψ₂n+1 = ψ₂n + αn·(ψ₁n+1 − ψ₁n), with αn itself allowed to change each step. That would match the repo's core update rule shape (ψn+1 = ψn + …) instead of treating influence as a one-time snapshot.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

Exact function f and coefficient α not derived. Symmetry (α₁₂ = α₂₁?) unspecified. Range (local vs. nonlocal) unspecified.
