Definition

Aligned-choice reinforcement: when two states share the same phase, their combination exceeds either alone.

Mathematics

A₁>0, A₂>0 (aligned phase) A_T = A₁ + A₂ Reinforcement: A_T > A₁ and A_T > A₂ (Firewall: this addition form records an aligned-choice outcome; it does not import quantum superposition ontology.)
Combined amplitude A_T exceeds either individual amplitude when both are phase-aligned — the defining reinforcement condition.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

Needs both Transfer (a real exchange happening) and Interaction Differential (something worth aligning) upstream. What sets a given interaction to resolve as aligned rather than opposed is an open frontier question logged here as CCD-04, not yet derived.

Recursive Reframing

Currently a static addition of two fixed amplitudes. A recursive form would let alignment itself be tested and reinforced step by step — e.g., a phase-locking iteration where each step nudges φn+1 toward alignment based on the current combined amplitude, closer to how real coupled-oscillator locking (see E-524 Kuramoto) actually behaves rather than an instantaneous aligned/not-aligned snapshot.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

Why an interaction resolves aligned vs. opposed is not derived (CCD-04, flagged as a frontier problem). Conditions for resonance between specific mode types not yet characterized.
