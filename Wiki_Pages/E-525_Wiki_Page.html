Definition

A detector is a sampling operator, not a collapse mechanism: R(t)=∫W(x)ψ(x,t)dx. Formalizes Ch9's real prose claim ('Field -> Measurement, not Measurement -> Field') with an actual equation.

Mathematics

R(t)=∫W(x)ψ(x,t)dx. Narrow detector (W≈delta function): R(t)≈ψ(x₀,t), a point-sample. Broad detector: R(t) is a weighted average.
A narrow detector point-sampling the field (blue) versus a broad detector averaging over a region (red) -- both reading an already-existing field, with no feedback arrow back into it.

Math Trail
