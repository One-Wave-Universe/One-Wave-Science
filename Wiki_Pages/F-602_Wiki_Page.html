Definition

The measured imbalance between two interacting states. This is the quantity that drives every subsequent interaction outcome in this appendix — nothing downstream fires unless D_ψ ≠ 0.

Mathematics

D_ψ = ψ₁ − ψ₂ D_ψ=0 → no imbalance, no interaction driven D_ψ≠0 → differential exists, interaction may proceed
D_psi as psi_1 sweeps past a fixed psi_2=1.0 — the interaction only 'switches on' away from the red line where D_psi=0.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

Directly downstream of F-601 (influence must exist for a differential to matter) and a specialization of A-103's general Differential definition (Δ(A,B)=A−B) applied specifically to two interacting states rather than a state and Ground/Zero.

Recursive Reframing

Currently a single snapshot comparison. A recursive version would track D_ψ as it evolves each step: D_ψn+1 = ψ₁n+1 − ψ₂n+1, letting F-603 Transfer downstream respond to a continuously-updating imbalance rather than one fixed value — this is close to what Transfer already needs (dQ/dt terms), so making it explicit here would tighten the chain.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

Only scalar differential defined; vector form deferred. Whether D_ψ is the only relevant differential, or additional components exist, is unresolved.
