Definition

The formal framework for individual Access Line behavior: persistence, merging, reactivation, strength, and directionality — the layer beneath B-213/214's count-and-depth math, addressing single-line behavior rather than the whole set.

Mathematics

Candidate persistence: S_i(t) = S_i(0)·exp(−λ_i·t) λ_i=0: pure persistence | λ_i>0: decay Candidate reinforcement: S_i(t+1) = S_i(t) + μ·usage(t) Fork: Flat Depth (d_k=d_0+k) vs. Weighted Depth (d_k=d_0+Σw_i)
Persistence curves for different decay rates lambda — lambda=0 (flat line) is B-213's original 'persistent' reading; any lambda>0 is real decay, not yet chosen between.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

Directly answers the question B-213 raises but doesn't resolve (do lines decay or persist?) with a real candidate form, and explicitly reconciles against B-214's growth formula rather than silently assuming they agree — the Flat-vs-Weighted fork is recorded, not resolved, here.

Recursive Reframing

Both candidate forms (persistence decay, reinforcement growth) are already stated as explicit recursive steps (S_i(t+1) as a function of S_i(t)) — the open work is choosing between them and fixing λ/μ, not making them recursive.

Yellow Audit — what's still open

λ_i not determined for any line. μ not derived or tested. Directionality representation (scalar/vector/paired) not chosen. Merge/collapse has no mathematical form proposed at all. Flat-vs-Weighted Depth fork unresolved, may require revising B-214.
