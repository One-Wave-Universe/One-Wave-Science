B-225: Five-Stage Field Transformation Cycle

The earlier contradiction is resolved. Field is the input/output envelope, not one of the five internal stages. Choice is the third internal stage and had been dropped from the later wording.

FIELDn → [ COMPRESSION → CENTER → CHOICE → EMERGENCE → EXPANSION ] → FIELDn+1
1. Compression
Reduce active possibilities toward a coherent reference.
2. Center
Meet the null/reference/pivot condition.
3. Choice
Select Compression or Expression bias.
4. Emergence
A selected displacement appears from center.
5. Expansion
Write the emerged pattern into the larger field.

Why the two older versions looked incompatible

The older sequence Compress → Center → Choose → Emerge → Expand named the five internal operations. The later sequence Field → Compression → Center → Emergence → Expansion → Field counted the surrounding field as though it were an internal phase and accidentally omitted Choice. They were not two rival cycles. They were two different accounting schemes, one of them bad at accounting.

Resolution: preserve Field as the pre/post context, restore Choice as stage three, and keep the five operational stages inside the field-to-field loop.

Yellow mathematics

χ_n = C(Ψ_n) ψ_(0,n) = Z(χ_n) σ_n ∈ {-1,+1} m_n ∈ {0,σ_n} Δψ_n = a_n m_n, a_n ≥ 0 ψ_(e,n) = ψ_(0,n) + Δψ_n Ψ_(n+1) = X(Ψ_n, ψ_(e,n))

Choice selects the polarity bias σn. Move either holds at zero or executes that bias. Emergence is the resulting displacement. Expansion writes the result into the next field.

12-1(0)-1-12 relationship

12n → 1 → 0 → 1 → 12n+1

The 12 values are the full field before and after the cycle; the two 1 values are compressed and emerged local states; 0 is the center. Choice acts at the center transition but is not an extra coordinate.

What remains open

The scale-specific physical forms of C, Z, and X are not yet derived. The amplitude limits are open. The exact two-choice and three-move closure claims remain inherited from B-224 and B-223. The numeric labels remain architectural mnemonics until derived.
