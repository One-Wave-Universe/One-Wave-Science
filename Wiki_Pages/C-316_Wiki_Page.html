C-316: Charge-Sign and Direction Conflation Inside Book 1 Chapter 11

The contradiction was sharper than “Chapter 4 versus Chapter 11.” Chapter 11 contradicted itself three paragraphs apart. Its prose agreed with Chapter 4; its later math used the wrong inward/compression label for the electron.

Old incorrect wording: electron = compression-dominant, inward, −Pc.
Correction: electron = negative boundary-pressure sign with an outward-pointing shell-boundary gradient. Sign and spatial direction are separate quantities.

Formal distinction

P_q = s_q P_c, s_q ∈ {-1,+1} G_q = ∇P_q

The sign sq is not the direction of the gradient Gq. Whole-mode Compression/Expression dominance would be a third variable and cannot be inferred from either one without an explicit model.

Corrected annihilation statement

e+ : +P_c, positive boundary-pressure sign; outward-propagating release e− : −P_c, negative boundary-pressure sign; outward-pointing shell-boundary gradient combined signed pressure: +P_c + (−P_c) = 0
