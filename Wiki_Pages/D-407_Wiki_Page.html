D-407: Harmonic Shell / Two-Shell Neutron Calibration Reanalysis

The caught mistake

Shell width is not shell spacing. R+ and R− are profile-center radii. σ is a radial width. The earlier use of 2πσ as a competing wavelength was a role conflation, not an independent calibration.
R+ = 0.7331 fm
R− = 0.8409 fm
σ  = 0.210 fm
center separation = 0.1078 fm
σ / separation = 1.9481

Adjacent-shell test

Assuming, but not yet deriving, that the two centers are adjacent harmonic shells:

n_est = R+/(R−−R+) = 6.800557
nearest adjacent integers: 7 and 8
λ+ = 2πR+/7 = 0.658029 fm
λ− = 2πR−/8 = 0.660441 fm
least-squares λ* = 0.659395 fm

Deeper energy result

R_n = nλ/(2π)
k_n = n/R_n = 2π/λ

The current D-405 family has the same tangential wavenumber at every shell number. A dispersion law ω(k) therefore cannot produce an n-dependent energy ladder by itself. D-405 quantizes radius geometry, not energy.

What still blocks carbon
