Gray — Standard Model Reference

The proton is a composite particle made of two up quarks and one down quark, bound by the strong force via gluon exchange. Charge: +1. Mass: 938.3 MeV/c^2. Spin: 1/2. The strong force is described by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Color charge: quarks carry one of three color charges (red, green, blue). Gluons carry color charge and mediate the strong force. The proton’s charge radius: approximately 0.84 fm. Confinement: quarks cannot be isolated — they are permanently confined inside hadrons. The QCD explanation of confinement involves the color force growing stronger with distance.

Standard Model strength: extremely precise predictions for proton structure and scattering. Standard Model limitation: confinement is not analytically proved from QCD first principles. The mass of the proton (~938 MeV) vastly exceeds the sum of its quark masses (~10 MeV). The remaining ~928 MeV comes from gluon field energy — not yet fully explained analytically.
