Definition

A 3D bounded resonant mode with angular structure -- in a spherically symmetric restoring field, modes may be classified by angular quantum numbers.

Mathematics

∇²ψ+k²ψ=0 (Helmholtz equation). ψ(r,θ,φ)=R(r)·Y_l^m(θ,φ). Angular quantum numbers l, m borrowed from standard spherical-harmonic form, not yet derived from One-Wave geometry.

Math Trail (dependency chain)

Directly downstream of D-402's periodicity condition, extended into three spatial dimensions -- the natural next step once a mode is known to be periodic at all, before it's known to have any particular angular shape.

Recursive Reframing

The Helmholtz equation itself is a standard eigenvalue problem, already implicitly recursive in the same sense as any mode expansion; nothing new to propose here beyond what D-401/402 already establish.
