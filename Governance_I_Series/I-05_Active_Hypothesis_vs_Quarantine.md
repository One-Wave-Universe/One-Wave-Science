# I-05 — Active Hypothesis, Proposed Build, and Quarantine Boundaries

Status: YELLOW — Governance Rule

## Purpose

One-Wave cannot investigate unknown mechanisms if every unproven idea is treated as rejected material. Lack of proof is not, by itself, a reason to exile a hypothesis from active work.

This rule separates four states that were previously being collapsed together:

1. **Active Hypothesis** — an unproven One-Wave proposal that remains open for derivation, simulation, comparison, or experiment.
2. **Proposed Build** — an engineering architecture offered for implementation and testing. It does not claim that nature, biology, or consciousness already uses the design.
3. **Disputed or Superseded Formulation** — a specific grounding, dependency, or wording that failed an audit, while the underlying question may remain active.
4. **Quarantined Source** — preserved historical material that must not be silently reintroduced as accepted canon because it contains unresolved contradictions, broken attribution, corrupted provenance, or a formulation already replaced by a clearer version.

## Governing Rule

> Unknown is not rejected. Hypothetical is not censored. A failed grounding invalidates that grounding, not automatically the entire research question.

A proposal must not be quarantined solely because:

- consciousness is not yet directly measurable,
- the mechanism is hypothetical,
- the mathematics is incomplete,
- the hardware has not yet been built,
- or the idea uses analogy while clearly labeling the analogy.

Those conditions require status labels and open tests, not erasure.

## Required Status Test

Before assigning Quarantined Lineage, answer:

1. Is the underlying question still open?
2. Did the audit reject the whole proposal, or only a specific grounding or dependency?
3. Can the proposal be restated as an active hypothesis without claiming proof?
4. Can any part be tested as a proposed build?
5. Does the material contain a contradiction, provenance problem, or superseded formulation that actually requires quarantine?

If the question remains open and the proposal can be stated without false certainty, use **Active Hypothesis** or **Proposed Build**, not quarantine.

## Consciousness Material

The M4 / Four-Mind / Five-Mind / Six-Mind / Dream Engine / Administrator material belongs in an active **Proposed One-Wave Consciousness** section.

Its status is:

- not established neuroscience,
- not a verified anatomy map,
- not a proven account of awareness,
- not accepted as settled physics,
- but still legitimate One-Wave hypothesis work.

Specific failed groundings remain recorded:

- Four Mind did not originally map cleanly to B-221 because BEGIN and BREAK were absent.
- Five Mind was incorrectly grounded in B-213, which is pairwise; A-111's multi-neighbor average is the stronger candidate grounding.
- Six Mind was only shape-matched to G-711; G-710's regulated-response function is the stronger candidate grounding.

Those corrections do not require deleting the consciousness hypothesis. They define what must be repaired and tested.

## Android Brain Material

The Android Brain belongs in a separate proposed engineering book.

It may use One-Wave consciousness hypotheses as design inspiration while making a narrower claim:

> This is a proposed architecture to build and test, not proof that the architecture creates consciousness or reproduces the human brain.

Functional labels such as Dream Engine, M4 weighing layer, and Administrator may be used in the proposed-build book as module names, provided every chapter distinguishes:

- module function,
- implementation proposal,
- consciousness hypothesis,
- and verified result.

I-03 still requires duplicate and lineage checks before quality review.

> A duplicate or disputed lineage check must not silently close an open hypothesis merely because one earlier grounding failed.

## Active Architecture Storage Rule

Active hypotheses do not receive a separate appendix letter or a generic
`Working_Hypotheses` sandbox. They remain in the proper A–G node, book chapter,
or scale-specific child node and carry the normal proof-stage label.

```text
A–G address = where the claim belongs
GREEN/YELLOW/BRONZE/... = how far the claim has been worked
```

Use GREEN for a clearly defined active hypothesis. Promote to YELLOW when the
variables, equations, assumptions, boundaries, and internal checks are built.
Promote to BRONZE only after a reproducible simulation is run with metadata and
a first successful validation under I-02.

Only provenance history, superseded formulations, and genuinely quarantined
sources belong outside the active architecture.

## Filing Rule

Use this disposition sequence:

```text
Exact duplicate at same scale -> merge or cross-reference
Failed grounding -> preserve correction; re-ground or leave unresolved
Open consciousness proposal -> Active Hypothesis section
Buildable architecture -> Proposed Build book
Contradictory or superseded source -> Quarantine / History
Verified or derived result -> normal proof lifecycle
```

## Final Principle

One-Wave must preserve the difference between claiming and exploring.

A framework that only stores what is already proven cannot discover anything. A framework that labels speculation as proof cannot be trusted. The working middle is active, clearly labeled hypothesis.
