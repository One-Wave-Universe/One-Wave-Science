# Chapter 4 - Sequence Routing and Spectral Stability

Status: PROPOSED BUILD / YELLOW CANDIDATES

## Purpose

This chapter separates route scheduling from movement memory and stability measurement.

## Route-Grammar Ladder

- Fibonacci word: fixed golden-ratio binary regression path.
- Sturmian: balanced aperiodic two-branch grammar.
- Episturmian: reversal-aware multi-symbol directive grammar.
- Arnoux-Rauzy: strict multi-route subclass with exact factor-complexity tests.
- Plastic/Padovan: candidate three-rail grammar for `n`, `2n`, and `2n+1`.

These words schedule or validate route-family attention. They do not directly command actuator position.

## Axis and Choice Separation

If `x_t` selects route family `d_x`, local choice supplies

\[
\Delta q_t=c_t\mathbf d_{x_t},\qquad c_t\in\{-1,0,+1\}.
\]

In the 2D triangular route test, three route axes correspond to six directed movements around one center. The `3:1` axis-pair view and `6:1` directed-neighbor view describe different counts of the same local geometry. Neither is automatically the `3:1` nerve aggregation ratio.

## Spectral Audit

Pisot-like recurrence is tested for dominant expression with contracting internal error. Salem-like recurrence is tested for expression, reciprocal return, and persistent unit-circle rhythm. Mahler measure audits total algebraic expansion.

These are measurements and controller-design comparisons, not foundational movement mechanisms.

## Advanced Mathematics Hold

Deninger determinants, Rodriguez-Villegas modular computations, and elliptic dilogarithms remain downstream tools. They become relevant only after a qualifying integer or Laurent-polynomial controller has been derived.

## Required Benchmark

Every proposed route/spectral controller must be compared on the same movement task against:

- periodic schedule;
- random schedule;
- learned schedule without word constraints;
- ordinary damped controller;
- Hopfield-only and Boltzmann-only memory;
- hybrid procedural architecture.

Measure task success, balance error, route coverage, recurrence gap, correction time, energy use, drift, and safety stops.

## Promotion Rule

Elegant number theory does not promote a movement architecture. Promotion requires reproducible improvement in the embodied simulation and a clear causal bridge from the mathematical property to the measured behavior.
