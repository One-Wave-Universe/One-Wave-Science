## Gray — Standard Model Reference

Antimatter consists of antiparticles — particles with the same mass but opposite quantum numbers.
The positron is the antiparticle of the electron: same mass (0.511 MeV/c^2), opposite charge (+1).
The antiproton is the antiparticle of the proton: same mass, charge -1.
Dirac predicted antimatter in 1928 from his relativistic quantum equation.
When matter meets antimatter, annihilation occurs: both convert to photons.
e+ + e- -> 2 gamma (511 keV photons)

The antimatter asymmetry problem:
The Big Bang should have produced equal amounts of matter and antimatter.
If so, they would have annihilated completely. Nothing would exist.
But the universe exists. There is far more matter than antimatter.
This matter-antimatter asymmetry is one of the deepest unsolved problems in physics.
CP violation (charge-parity symmetry violation) is measured but insufficient
to explain the observed asymmetry.

Standard Model strength: antimatter exists and is well described by QFT.
Antihydrogen has been produced and measured at CERN.
Standard Model limitation: the antimatter asymmetry problem is unsolved.
Why the universe has matter at all is unexplained.
