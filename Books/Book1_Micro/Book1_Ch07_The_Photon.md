## Gray — Standard Model Reference

The photon is the force-carrier particle of electromagnetism.
Mass: 0. Charge: 0. Spin: 1.
The photon travels at c = 299,792,458 m/s in vacuum.
The photon exhibits wave-particle duality — it behaves as a wave (interference, diffraction)
and as a particle (photoelectric effect, Compton scattering).
Photon energy: E = hf = hbar * omega.
Photon momentum: p = h/lambda = hbar * k.
The photon is its own antiparticle.
In quantum field theory, the photon is an excitation of the electromagnetic field.

Standard Model strength: QED (quantum electrodynamics) is the most precisely tested theory
in physics. The photon mediates all electromagnetic interactions.
Standard Model limitation: wave-particle duality remains philosophically unresolved.
Why light travels at exactly c is explained by Maxwell's equations but not derived
from a deeper principle. The photon mass being exactly zero is assumed, not derived.
