## Gray — Standard Model Reference

The atomic nucleus consists of protons and neutrons (nucleons) bound by the strong nuclear force.
The strong force is short-range (< 1 fm) and attractive between all nucleons.
Nuclear binding energy: the mass of a nucleus is less than the sum of its constituent nucleons.
The binding energy per nucleon peaks at iron-56 (~8.8 MeV/nucleon).
Nuclear shell model: nucleons occupy quantized shells analogous to electron shells.
Magic numbers (2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126) correspond to closed shells with extra stability.
Nuclear forces are residual strong forces — the actual strong force acts between quarks.
The nuclear force is mediated by pion exchange (Yukawa potential).

Standard Model strength: nuclear shell model and liquid drop model give good predictions.
Standard Model limitation: nuclear force is a residual effect — not a fundamental force.
Why specific magic numbers correspond to extra stability is described but not deeply explained.
Nuclear binding from first principles (QCD) remains an unsolved problem.

## 2D One-Wave Interpretation

In 2D, the nucleus is a cluster of braided loops packed together.

Each nucleon (proton or neutron) is a braided loop (Chapters 2 and 6).
When multiple loops are packed together, they couple (E-04).
The coupled cluster finds a minimum-energy configuration —
the same principle that makes hexagonal cells pack efficiently (E-03).

The nucleus is not a bag of billiard balls.
It is a cluster of mutually coupled Persistent Modes
that find stable lock-in configurations through resonance (F-04).

Magic numbers in 2D:
Closed-shell configurations are the ones where all the loops
fit together without any dangling coupling ends.
2, 8, 20, 28... are the numbers where the coupled loop cluster
forms a complete symmetric configuration.
Same logic as why noble gases are chemically inert:
all their shell modes are fully occupied and balanced.

## 3D One-Wave Interpretation

In 3D, the nucleus is a braided mode cluster with collective resonance.

Each nucleon contributes its own three-vortex braid structure.
When nucleons couple, their braids interlock at the boundaries.
The interlocking reduces the total surface energy (E-03):
two surfaces merge into one shared boundary.
This surface energy reduction is the nuclear binding energy.

Nuclear binding energy = total surface energy reduction from interlocking braids

The binding energy per nucleon peaks at iron-56 because:
At iron-56, the surface-to-volume ratio of the nucleus reaches its minimum.
Adding more nucleons beyond iron-56 increases the surface energy
faster than the volume coupling energy decreases.
This is the same physics as bubble coalescence —
small bubbles merge to reduce surface area, but there is a limit.

Nuclear shell model:
The magic numbers arise from the harmonic shell condition (D-05) applied to the nucleus.
The nucleus has its own harmonic shells — not the same as atomic shells,
but governed by the same 2*pi*R = n*lambda condition at nuclear scale.
Closed shells at magic numbers have zero net coupling overhang —
all modes are fully coupled, no dangling ends.

Fission and fusion:
Fission: a heavy nucleus (above iron) splits to reduce surface energy.
The split reduces total surface-to-volume ratio.
This releases the excess surface tension energy as kinetic energy of the fragments.

Fusion: light nuclei merge to reduce total surface.
Below iron, merging always reduces total surface energy.
That energy release is fusion energy.

No pion exchange required.
The nuclear force is direct lattice coupling between neighboring braided modes (beta term).
The range (~1 fm) is set by the lattice coupling length.

## Mathematics

Nuclear binding energy from surface reduction (E-03):
B_nuclear = E_surface(separated) - E_surface(bound)
B_nuclear = sum_i(sigma * 4*pi*R_i^2) - sigma * 4*pi*R_nucleus^2

Nuclear radius scaling:
R_nucleus ~ R_0 * A^(1/3)
where A = mass number, R_0 ~ 1.2 fm (empirical, derivation deferred)

Surface energy of nucleus:
E_surface = sigma * 4*pi*R_nucleus^2 = sigma * 4*pi*R_0^2 * A^(2/3)

Binding energy per nucleon (liquid drop analog):
B/A ~ a_V - a_S * A^(-1/3) - a_C * Z^2 * A^(-4/3) - a_A * (N-Z)^2 / A
where a_V, a_S, a_C, a_A are volume, surface, Coulomb, asymmetry terms
One-Wave: a_S term comes from E-03, a_V term from E-04 coupling

Magic number shell closure (D-05 at nuclear scale):
2*pi*R_shell = n*lambda_nuclear
Magic numbers correspond to n values where all shells are fully occupied.

Coupling between nucleons (E-04):
E_coupling = sum_{i,j} c_ij * psi_i * psi_j
Short range: c_ij ~ 0 for |r_i - r_j| > lambda_nuclear
