## Core Principle

```text
Healthy unit = stable identity + clear boundary + unused adaptive capacity
```

A balanced self is not perfectly motionless. It knows where it stands while
remaining open to change.

Too little boundary allows the system to dissolve into surrounding pressure.
Too much boundary locks the system into rigidity. Too little unused capacity
turns every new demand into overload.

## Individual Self

"Know yourself" does not mean dividing into a controller and a controlled
prisoner. It means maintaining enough reference to recognize:

- current state,
- needs,
- limits,
- expressive drive,
- compressive restraint,
- and chosen direction.

Self-control means internal coordination, not suppression by one imagined
internal ruler.

## Yellow State Model

The earlier single 0–100 scale mixed energy, direction, and integrity. They are
now separate:

```text
Theta_n = (q_n, a_n, p_n)
```

where:

- `q in [0,1]` is internal coherence or retained access,
- `a in [0,1]` is total activation,
- `p in [-1,1]` is compressive-to-expressive polarity.

This makes several combinations possible:

```text
low activation + high coherence = rest / sleep / standby
high activation + high coherence = intense but organized action
high activation + falling coherence = overload danger
low activation + low coherence = possible collapse or lost access
```

Low energy is therefore not automatically failure, and compression is not
identical to low energy.

## Provisional Activation Windows

The current bands are normalized design scaffolds, not constants:

```text
90-100   overload / danger / reset candidate

60-70    upper modulation pivot
about 50 reference operating center
30-40    lower modulation pivot / cooling / recovery
0-10     very low activation; meaning depends on coherence q
```

Polarity remains a separate axis at every activation level.

## Modulation Law

Let `Theta*` be the selected reference state and

```text
e_n = Theta_n - Theta*_n.
```

A simple restoring command is

```text
u_n = -K e_n,
Theta_(n+1) = Pi_Omega(Theta_n + u_n),
```

with diagonal gains satisfying

```text
0 < k_q, k_a, k_p < 2.
```

Then each local error decays as

```text
e_(j,n+1) = (1-k_j)e_(j,n).
```

This provides an internally stable Yellow model without claiming that the gains
or numerical windows are biologically universal.

G-713 gives the bounded action-selection version for Hold, Increase, Decrease,
Redirect, Stabilize, Reject, and Admit.

## Balanced Share Illustration

If two participants provisionally share an active budget of 85, an equal split
is

```text
85 / 2 = 42.5.
```

This is only an allocation illustration. It does not mean a person is half a
self. A healthy connection still requires each side to retain identity, agency,
and room for change.

## Scale Question

The same architecture may recur in:

- a person,
- two people,
- a group,
- a neural subsystem,
- an AI architecture,
- a Wave Computer cell,
- or a larger lattice mode.

Under I-04, the recurrence remains a candidate architectural homology until
scale-specific variables and rescaling laws are supplied. This chapter is
Yellow because its internal state and modulation math are explicit, not because
cross-scale physical identity has been proven.
