# Chapter 3 — Meet in the Middle and Shared Access

Status: ACTIVE HYPOTHESIS / RELATIONAL APPLICATION

Two independent systems do not need to become identical to connect. They require enough compatibility in:

- timing,
- speed,
- intensity,
- direction,
- and phase.

A proposed shared access line forms through:

```text
synchronization + mutually accepted phase shift
```

The governing relationship is:

> No control but self-control.

Each side controls its own movement, timing, intensity, participation, and response. Coercion is not synchronization.

Possible movements include:

- both approach,
- one approaches while the other holds,
- both hold,
- one or both retreat,
- one rejects,
- or both create a new shared state.

The relationship exists only where independent choices overlap.

This chapter may apply to hemispheric coordination, human relationships, teams, human-AI collaboration, and distributed machines. These are separate scale instances and must not be treated as physically identical without a scale mapping.
